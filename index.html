Research

My research interests include: Natural Language Processing, Machine Learning, Deep Learning, GANs, making sense of the learning inside the black-box, and Hierarchal Temporal Memory

I've conducted research as an entry to the Intel Science and Engineering Fair. The purpose of the project was to use NLP methods (Text Vectorization, N-Grams, TF-IDF, Linear Support Vector Machines, Stochastic Gradient Descent Optimization, and Coordinate Descent Optimization on the Wolfe Dual form of the SVM) on Windows API System Calls to detect malware. Our model achieved a highest of 96% accuracy and we were named to be Intel ISEF finalists. The project also won the Yale Science and Engineering Award, 4th place Grand Award at Intel ISEF, and special awards from numerous organizations.

The paper can be found here: (arXiv) NtMalDetect: A Machine Learning Approach to Malware Detection Using Native API System Calls

I self-studied Linear Algebra and Multivariable calculus on top of finishing AP Calculus BC at school and finished Andrew Ng's courses to make all this happen. I am now comfortable with much of the theoretical foundations as well as using TensorFlow and other Python modules. I am currently looking for research opportunities and willing to devote much of my time to research. Please contact me if there are such opportunities.
